Executive Summary

This document is a formal project proposal and service agreement submitted by the Service Provider to Newbox Greens Ventures, operating under the brand Choccoladd, for the design, development, and delivery of a complete official brand website.

Choccoladd is a premium Malaysian cocoa-derived product brand established in June 2024 in collaboration with Lembaga Koko Malaysia (Malaysian Cocoa Board). The website will serve as the brand's primary digital presence — showcasing food products, gift sets, non-food skincare items, brand story, company profile, and providing a direct channel for customer inquiries and orders via WhatsApp.

The Service Provider proposes to design and build the website from the ground up using modern web technologies, ensuring the site is fully responsive across all devices, professionally branded, and ready for immediate deployment upon final sign-off.

Project Objectives

  • Establish a professional, conversion-focused digital presence for the Choccoladd brand
  • Showcase the full product catalogue — food products, gift sets, and non-food items — with clear pricing and descriptions
  • Enable direct customer ordering via WhatsApp integration on every product listing
  • Communicate the brand story, vision, and mission to build customer trust and credibility
  • Provide a functional contact form for business and customer inquiries
  • Deliver a fully mobile-responsive website optimised for all screen sizes
  • Deploy the website on a reliable hosting platform with version-controlled source code

Clause 1 — Scope of Services

1.1 Included Services

The Service Provider will design, develop, and deliver the website as described in Section 02 of this proposal. All listed features and pages will be delivered within the agreed timeline, subject to timely provision of content by the Client.

1.2 Excluded Services

  • Domain name purchase (Client's responsibility unless otherwise agreed in writing)
  • Ongoing hosting fees beyond the initial deployment setup
  • Content creation such as product photography or professional copywriting
  • Third-party integrations not listed in the agreed scope (e.g. e-commerce payment gateway, inventory management system)
  • Search engine optimisation (SEO) campaigns or paid digital advertising
Clause 2 — Client Responsibilities

The Client agrees to:

  • Provide all required content — including images, product details, pricing, social media handles, SSM number, and operating hours — in a timely manner
  • Review deliverables and provide written feedback within 5 business days of each submission
  • Designate a single authorised point of contact for all project communications
  • Make all payments in accordance with the agreed payment schedule
  • Ensure all provided images and content are owned by the Client or properly licensed for commercial use
Clause 3 — Revisions Policy
  • This agreement includes 3 rounds of revisions per major section at no additional cost
  • A revision is defined as a minor change (text, colour, layout, or content adjustment) within the originally agreed scope
  • Additional revisions beyond the 3 included rounds will be quoted at RM [___] per hour
  • New features, additional pages, or changes outside the original scope will be treated as new work and quoted separately
Clause 4 — Timeline & Delays
  • The Service Provider will make all reasonable efforts to meet the agreed delivery schedule
  • Should the Client delay the provision of required content by more than 14 calendar days, the project timeline will be extended accordingly with no liability on the Service Provider
  • Delays caused by third-party services (hosting providers, domain registrars, payment processors) are beyond the Service Provider's control
Clause 5 — Payment Terms
  • The 50% deposit is due upon signing of this Agreement. Work will not commence until the deposit is received.
  • All payments are non-refundable once the corresponding phase of work has commenced
  • Invoices are payable within 3 business days of issuance
  • Late payment beyond 7 days may result in a pause in project delivery until full payment is received
  • Full ownership of the website will transfer to the Client only upon receipt of the final payment in full
Clause 6 — Intellectual Property
  • Upon receipt of full payment, the Client will own all custom code, design assets, and content created specifically for this project
  • The Service Provider retains the right to display this project in a professional portfolio unless otherwise agreed in writing
  • All third-party libraries, fonts, and frameworks used will remain subject to their respective open-source or commercial licences
  • The Client warrants that all provided logos, images, and content are owned by or properly licensed to the Client
Clause 7 — Confidentiality

Both parties agree to keep strictly confidential all sensitive business information shared during this engagement, including but not limited to pricing strategies, business plans, customer data, and proprietary processes. This obligation will survive termination of this Agreement.

Clause 8 — Post-Launch Support
  • The Service Provider will provide 30 days of complimentary bug-fix support following the official launch date
  • Bug-fix support covers issues directly resulting from the original development work only
  • New features, content updates, or design changes requested after launch will be billable at the agreed hourly rate
  • Monthly maintenance retainers are available upon request and will be quoted separately
Clause 9 — Termination
  • Either party may terminate this Agreement with 7 days written notice
  • Upon termination by the Client, all work completed to date will be invoiced and must be settled before any files or access are transferred
  • Upon termination by the Service Provider due to non-payment or material breach, the deposit will be forfeited and all work product will remain with the Service Provider
Clause 10 — Limitation of Liability

The Service Provider's total liability under this Agreement will not exceed the total fees paid by the Client. The Service Provider will not be liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential damages arising from the use or inability to use the website, including but not limited to loss of business, revenue, or data.

Clause 11 — Governing Law

This Agreement will be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Malaysia. Any disputes arising from this Agreement will be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Malaysia.
